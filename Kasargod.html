Kasaragode

Hosting numerous forts, wildlife sanctuaries, temple protected by a ‘croc’ - say the locals and magnificent hills, Kasaragod is uptown in all aspects. The ultimate traveling destination, has it all to enchant you. The natives are very friendly and the harmony between people of various religions is to be highlighted. A rare district in India housing seven languages and vivid appreciable cultures. Also, experience the Malabar coastal winds you have been reading about in Geography all this while. Get set to be awestruck and jaw dropped to find manmade and nature blend at its best at Kasaragod.

About Kasargod


Kasaragod or Kaasrodu is a municipal town and administrative headquarters of Kasaragod district of Kerala state in India. Situated in the rich biodiversity of Western Ghats, it is known for the Chandragiri and Bekal Forts Chandragiri River, historic Kolathiri Rajas, natural environment of Ranipuram and Kottancheri Hills, historical and religious sites like the Madiyan Kulom temple, Madhur Temple, Ananthapuram Lake Temple and Malik Deenar Mosque. It is located 50 km south of the major port city & a commercial hub Mangalore and 578 km north of state capital Thiruvanathapuram. Ranipuram is a hill station and a major tourist attraction in the Kasaragod district
